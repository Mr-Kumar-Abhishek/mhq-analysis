# Global MHQ Benchmarks (2023-2024)

These benchmarks are based on the **Mental State of the World** reports by Sapien Labs (Global Mind Project), reflecting data from over 1 million respondents across 80+ countries.

## 1. Global Averages

| Year | Global Average MHQ | Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **2023** | **65 / 300** | Stabilized at post-pandemic lows. |
| **2024** | **63 / 300** | Slight decline; no significant recovery to pre-pandemic levels. |

*   **Distressed or Struggling**: ~27% of the global population.
*   **Succeeding or Thriving**: ~38% of the global population.

---

## 2. Age-Based Benchmarks

There is a significant and widening gap between younger and older generations.

| Age Group | Average MHQ Score (2024) | % Distressed/Struggling |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **18–24** | **38** | ~41% |
| **25–34** | **~45** | High risk |
| **35–54** | **~70** | Managing |
| **55–64** | **101** | Succeeding (Pre-pandemic norm) |
| **65+** | **~115** | Thriving |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Younger generations (18-24) are 4-5 times more likely to report mental health challenges compared to the 65+ demographic.

---

## 3. Geographic Benchmarks (Highs & Lows)

Mental wellbeing does not strictly correlate with economic wealth. In fact, many non-English speaking developing nations score higher than developed Anglosphere nations.

### Top Scoring Countries
1.  **Dominican Republic**
2.  **Sri Lanka**
3.  **Tanzania**
4.  **Panama**
5.  **Malaysia**

### Lower Scoring Regions/Countries
*   **Core Anglosphere**: (UK, Australia, USA) consistently rank in the lower half.
*   **United Kingdom**: Often ranks among the lowest in mental wellbeing relative to its economic status.

---

## 4. Key Factors Influencing Benchmarks

*   **Digital Connectivity**: Younger age of first smartphone ownership is strongly correlated with lower MHQ scores in adulthood.
*   **Diet**: Consumption of ultra-processed foods is linked to poorer mental health outcomes.
*   **Social Fabric**: Countries with stronger family ties and social cohesion (often in Latin America and Africa) tend to have higher average MHQ scores.
*   **Economic Correlation**: Wealthier countries often report lower social self-scores and higher levels of stress.
