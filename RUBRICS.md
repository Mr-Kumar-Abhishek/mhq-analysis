# MHQ Scoring Rubrics

The Mental Health Quotient (MHQ) is a comprehensive metric of mental wellbeing. It follows a non-linear scoring system that ranges from **-100 to +200**.

## 1. Overall MHQ Score Categories

The composite score is categorized into five functional states:

| Score Range | Category | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **-100 to 0** | **Distressed / Struggling** | High risk for clinical mental disorders. Significant functional impairment in daily life. |
| **1 to 50** | **Enduring** | Individuals not at clinical risk but facing persistent challenges or low mental energy. |
| **51 to 100** | **Managing** | Stable mental functioning. Generally productive and able to handle daily stressors. |
| **101 to 150** | **Succeeding** | High level of mental wellbeing. Positive outlook and strong social/cognitive functioning. |
| **151 to 200** | **Thriving** | Optimal mental health. Exceptional resilience, drive, and emotional regulation. |

---

## 2. Dimensional Sub-scores

The MHQ evaluates 6 key dimensions. Each dimension has its own sub-score ranging from **-50 to +100**.

### 2.1 Mood & Outlook
Reflects the ability to manage emotions and maintain an optimistic perspective.
*   **High Score**: Positive, stable, and hopeful.
*   **Low Score**: Persistent sadness, anxiety, or negativity.

### 2.2 Social Self
Reflects how an individual interacts with others and perceives themselves in a social context.
*   **High Score**: Strong relationships, high self-worth.
*   **Low Score**: Social withdrawal, low self-esteem.

### 2.3 Drive & Motivation
Measures the capacity to pursue goals and complete daily activities.
*   **High Score**: High energy, persistence, clear goals.
*   **Low Score**: Apathy, lack of focus, difficulty starting tasks.

### 2.4 Cognition
Assesses basic cognitive functions and complex comprehension.
*   **High Score**: Sharp focus, clear thinking, long-term perspective.
*   **Low Score**: Confusion, forgetfulness, inability to focus.

### 2.5 Adaptability & Resilience
Evaluates the ability to cope with life's challenges and changes.
*   **High Score**: Highly adaptive, bounces back from setbacks.
*   **Low Score**: Rigid behavior, easily overwhelmed by change.

### 2.6 Mind-Body Connection
Focuses on the regulation of physical states by mental processes.
*   **High Score**: Good sleep, physical vitality, balanced appetite.
*   **Low Score**: Physical manifestations of stress (insomnia, fatigue).

---

## 3. Scoring Logic Summary

*   **Non-linear Weighting**: Negative responses (problems/symptoms) are weighted more heavily than positive responses (assets/capabilities) to better identify clinical risk.
*   **Normalization**: Raw scores are normalized against global benchmarks to ensure a 10-point shift has the same functional meaning across the entire scale.
